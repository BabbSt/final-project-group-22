Level 1: Alevin

-Alevin live in gravel in their redd
-They get all their nutrients from the yolk sac
-The yolk sac lasts for about 4-6 weeks
-Fish and birds are predators of alevin
-An alevin's growth depends on the oxygen levels and temperature of the water

Level 2: Fry

-Fry are twice as big as alevins
-They have teeth and scales
-Fry eat plankton. As they get bigger, they eat insects
-Fry develop parr marks which help to camouflage them
-Fry swim in schools

Level 3: Smolt

-Smolts migrate from fresh water to salt water
-They spend many weeks in the estuary learning how to process salt water through their gills
-They grow larger in the estuary
-If an estuary floods, the smolt can be released into the saltwater too early and may die

Level 4: Adult

-When a smolt enters the ocean, it becomes an adult salmon
-Adult salmon eat herring, crustaceans, and squid
-Different types of salmon stay in the ocean for different amounts of time
-Some of the salmon's ocean predators include: dolphins, sharks, whales, and humans

Level 5: Spawners

-Spawners return to the estuary and get used to being back in fresh water
-Once in fresh water, thier bodies start to shut down. They stop eating
-Males develop another set of teeth and in most Pacific Northwest salmon, their bodies turn red
-The female builds the redds to lay her eggs
-A male has about 16 seconds to put milt on the eggs
-Bears, otters, and birds hunt for the salmon returning in the rivers and streams
